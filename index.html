The Challenge

It isn't plastic. It's plastic that's never economically collected.

India generates over 4 million tonnes of plastic waste every year. A large share enters formal and informal recycling systems — but millions of tonnes still escape effective collection, polluting streets, drains, rivers, and ecosystems across the country.

Lightweight single-use carry bags are used every day by millions of grocery stores, fruit & vegetable vendors, flower vendors, and self-employed micro retailers — because they remain the lowest-cost packaging option available.

Once discarded, these bags have little recovery value. That makes collection economically difficult, and leaves them outside the recycling system entirely — where the real damage begins.

The challenge is not awareness.
The challenge is replacing plastic where it is used the most.

Why Existing Alternatives Don't Scale

Certified compostable bags already exist. The challenge is affordability.

For millions of low-margin micro retailers, even a small increase in packaging cost directly affects daily earnings. Sustainable alternatives struggle at scale — not because they're unavailable, but because they aren't economically accessible at the point of use.

This is where corporate leadership creates measurable change — by making sustainable alternatives affordable at the point of use, CSR can help replace millions of conventional plastic carry bags with certified compostable alternatives.

Our Solution

We don't run campaigns. We replace plastic where it's consumed.

Using corporate CSR funding, we support the manufacturing of CPCB-certified compostable carry bags through our nationwide network of 100+ manufacturing partners.

Our 150+ Field Distribution Partners identify eligible small retailers, manage last-mile delivery, maintain distribution records, and collect project data for transparent reporting.

Retailers receive the bags at no product cost and pay only a nominal delivery charge — removing the biggest barrier to adopting sustainable packaging. Each bag carries a simple CSR project acknowledgment of the supporting organization.
